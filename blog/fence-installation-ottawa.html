Fence Installation in Ottawa: Styles, Materials & Cost

A good fence adds privacy, security, and curb appeal. Here's how to choose the right style, what affects fencing cost, and what to sort out before installation in the Ottawa area.

Common fence materials

  • Vinyl (PVC) — clean, modern look with virtually no maintenance. Great for full privacy and holds up well to Ottawa winters.
  • Wood — classic and cost-effective, with lots of style options. Needs periodic sealing or staining to stay looking sharp.
  • Chain-link — the most economical option for security and pet containment when privacy isn't the priority.
  • Aluminum — a premium, decorative option that's strong and low-maintenance.

What affects fencing cost

  • Linear footage — the total length is the biggest single driver.
  • Height and privacy — taller, full-privacy fences use more material.
  • Material choice — vinyl and aluminum cost more upfront than wood or chain-link.
  • Gates — each gate adds hardware and labour.
  • Ground conditions — rock, slopes, and tree roots can affect post installation.

Property lines, permits & locates

Before any posts go in, it's important to confirm property lines and arrange a utility locate so digging is safe. Some fences may require a permit depending on height and location. We help you plan these details up front to avoid surprises.

Posts set for the freeze/thaw cycle

Like decks, fence posts need to be set properly for Ottawa's freeze/thaw cycle so the fence stays straight and solid for years. Proper post depth and setting is what separates a fence that lasts from one that leans.
