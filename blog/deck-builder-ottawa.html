Building a Deck in Ottawa: Materials, Cost & What to Expect

A well-built deck adds usable living space and real value to your home. Here's how to think about materials, what actually drives the cost, and what the process looks like from first call to final walkthrough in the Ottawa area.

Choosing your decking material

The material you pick affects look, maintenance, and budget more than anything else. The three most common choices in Ottawa:

  • Pressure-treated wood — the most budget-friendly option. Durable and proven in our climate, but needs periodic staining/sealing to look its best.
  • Cedar — naturally beautiful with a warm tone and good resistance to rot. A mid-range option that still benefits from sealing.
  • Composite — the lowest-maintenance choice. Higher upfront cost, but no staining and excellent longevity, which many homeowners prefer long-term.

What drives deck cost

Like interlock, deck pricing isn't a flat per-square-foot number. The biggest cost factors are:

  • Size and height — larger decks and raised decks need more material and structural support.
  • Footings and framing — proper frost-depth footings are essential in Ottawa to prevent heaving during freeze/thaw.
  • Steps and levels — wrap-around steps and multi-level layouts add framing and labour.
  • Railings — aluminum, glass, or wood railings each carry different costs.
  • Site access and grading — a sloped or hard-to-reach yard increases the work involved.

Permits and frost footings

Most decks in Ottawa require a building permit, and footings must reach below the frost line so the structure stays level through winter. We handle the details so your deck is built to code and built to last.

What to expect during the build

After we confirm the design and scope, a typical deck moves through layout and footings, framing, decking, then steps and railings. We keep the jobsite clean and walk you through the finished build so everything meets your expectations.
